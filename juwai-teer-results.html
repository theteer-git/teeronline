How to Interpret Juwai Teer FR, SR and Historical Statistics

The Juwai Teer result should be read by checking the displayed date, First Round value, Second Round value and publication status together. A two-digit number without its date or round can be misunderstood, especially when visitors are comparing today's result with older records.

First Round and Second Round Are Separate Results

FR means First Round and SR means Second Round. Each round is published independently, so the same two-digit number appearing in different rounds or on different dates represents a separate result record.

Why Two-Digit Formatting Matters

Juwai Teer results range from 00 to 99. Leading zeroes are preserved because values such as 04 and 40 are different. Removing the zero would change the result and make historical comparisons inaccurate.

Understanding Recent Results

Historical Frequency and Repeated Numbers

House Digits and Ending Digits

For a two-digit result, the first digit is commonly described as the house digit and the second as the ending digit. For example, in 47, the house digit is 4 and the ending digit is 7. These labels help organize historical data but do not guarantee a future result.

Hot, Cold and Missing Numbers

Hot numbers generally refer to values that appeared more frequently in a selected historical period, while cold or missing numbers appeared less often or not at all. The meaning depends on the date range being reviewed and should be treated as descriptive, not predictive.

Repeated FR–SR Pairs

Historical records may show the same FR and SR pair more than once. A repeated pair confirms only that the combination occurred previously; it does not prove that the same pair is due to return.

Weekday and Date Comparisons
