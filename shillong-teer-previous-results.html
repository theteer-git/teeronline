Understanding Historical Shillong Teer FR and SR Records

Historical Shillong Teer results become more useful when they are interpreted correctly. Every archived row represents one completed playing date and contains two independent values: the First Round (FR) and the Second Round (SR). These values should always be read together with the corresponding date rather than as isolated numbers.

Reading FR and SR Correctly

FR and SR belong to different rounds of the same day. Even if the same two-digit number appears multiple times across different dates or rounds, each occurrence remains an independent historical record. Comparing only the number without checking the date can lead to incorrect conclusions.

Leading Zeroes Matter

The archive preserves leading zeroes because two-digit formatting is part of the historical record. Numbers such as 01, 09 and 03 are distinct values and should never be shortened to 1, 9 or 3 when comparing archived entries.
