Teer Common Numbers - Mathematical Approach to Digit Prediction

Shillong Teer Common Numbers and Khanapara Teer Common Numbers are derived using a unique digit complement logic. The formula is simple: take all four digits from today's First Round (FR) and Second Round (SR) results, add their complements (0↔5, 1↔6, 2↔7, 3↔8, 4↔9), and then find the missing digits from 0-9. These missing digits become the "Common Numbers" or "Common Digits" for the next day's game.

How Does the Common Number Calculation Work?

For example, if today's Shillong Teer result is 45-92, the digits are: 4,5,9,2. Adding complements: 4→9,5→0,9→4,2→7. So we have digits: 4,5,9,2,0,7. The missing digits from 0-9 are 1,3,6,8. These four digits become the predicted common digits for tomorrow. If any of these digits appear as the house (first digit) or ending (second digit) in tomorrow's FR or SR, the prediction is considered successful.

Why Use Common Numbers for Teer?

Many Teer enthusiasts use common numbers to narrow down their number selection. By focusing on house numbers (tens place) and ending numbers (units place) derived from these common digits, players can reduce hundreds of possible combinations to just a handful. The Juwai Teer Common Numbers and Shillong Morning Common Numbers follow the exact same mathematical principle.
