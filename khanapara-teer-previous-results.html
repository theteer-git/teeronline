How to Interpret Khanapara Teer Historical FR and SR Records

The archive should be read as a date-wise record of completed Khanapara Teer results. Every entry contains a playing date, weekday, First Round value and Second Round value. These fields work together: a number without its date and round can easily be misunderstood.

First Round and Second Round Are Separate Records

FR and SR are published independently. A number appearing in FR on one date is not the same record as the same number appearing in SR on another date. When comparing old results, always identify the round before making any historical observation.

Why Leading Zeroes Must Be Preserved

Khanapara Teer results use two-digit values from 00 to 99. The archive preserves leading zeroes because 03 and 30 are different records. Removing a leading zero would change the meaning of the historical entry.

Understanding Historical Frequency

A number that appears several times in the archive may be described as frequent within the selected records. A number with fewer appearances may be described as less frequent. These descriptions summarize past data only and do not establish what will happen in a future round.

Repeated Numbers and Pairs

Repeated numbers, same-day combinations and recurring FR–SR pairs can be identified from the archive. Their presence shows that a combination occurred before; it does not prove that the same combination will return.

Weekday Comparisons

The weekday filter helps visitors compare records from Mondays, Tuesdays and other playing days. Such comparisons can be useful for organizing historical research, but a weekday alone cannot determine a future result.

Missing or Unavailable Historical Dates

A date may be absent because the game was not scheduled, the result was unavailable, or the record has not yet been verified. The archive should not invent values to fill a missing date.

Using the Archive Responsibly

The archive is intended for checking, comparison and research. It should not be used as a guarantee, betting instruction or promise of a future FR or SR result.
